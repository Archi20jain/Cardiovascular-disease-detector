What is a Cardiovascular Attack?

A cardiovascular attack, commonly known as a heart attack, occurs when the blood flow to the heart is significantly reduced or blocked. This is usually due to a buildup of fat, cholesterol, and other substances, which form a plaque in the arteries.

The damage can be life-threatening if not treated quickly. Warning signs include chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, and cold sweats. With timely medical attention and tools like CardioDetect, early detection is now possible.

What Are the Early Warning Signs of a Heart Attack?

Early heart attack signs include chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, and cold sweats. Pain may spread to the arms, neck, or jaw. Quick action can prevent serious damage.
