About Cardiovascular Attack

A cardiovascular attack, commonly referred to as a heart attack, occurs when blood flow to the heart is suddenly blocked, often due to a blood clot or buildup of fatty deposits (plaque) in the coronary arteries. This blockage prevents oxygen-rich blood from reaching parts of the heart muscle, leading to damage or death of heart tissue. Symptoms may include chest pain, shortness of breath, dizziness, or discomfort in other areas like the arms or jaw. Prompt medical attention is crucial to minimize damage and improve outcomes.

Understanding Cardiovascular Attack Symptoms

Common signs include intense chest pain or discomfort, often described as pressure or squeezing, that may spread to the arms, back, neck, or jaw. Other symptoms can include shortness of breath, nausea, sweating, lightheadedness, or a feeling of impending doom. These symptoms can vary, especially between men and women, and not all heart attacks involve severe chest pain. Immediate medical attention can be lifesaving.
